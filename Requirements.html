1.4 Audience

The targeted audience for this product is beer pong players. Players must have access to the internet, and must attend an Ontario, post-secondary institution. Each team consists of at least one user. A user is registered with Pong Tracker. Guests are allowed to participate in a game. A guest is not registered with Pong Tracker.

1.5 Product Scope

Pong Tracker is a website that provides users the chance to record and view analytics of beer pong games played by themselves and fellow Pong Tracker users. The website allows users to enter every detail of the game they are playing while they are playing the game. The scope of this project includes both a traditional, computer web browser accessible web interface, as well as a mobile accessible website. The scope does not include native applications for any particular mobile device.

1.5.1 Contributions to Society

As a website that allows users to come together by post-graduate institution to record and share their Beer Pong statistics, Pong Tracker feeds the users' need to stay connected, meet new people, and creates an opportunity to share their interest of the game. Pong Tracker introduces the collaboration of social media with in-person interaction.

1.5.2 Contributions to Individuals

Pong Tracker creates a friendly, competitive and entertaining environment for every user and guest involved.

2 Product Description

2.1 Product Perspective

2.1.1 Product Overview

Pong Tracker is a web-based utility for tracking statistics of a given user's beer pong performance. It collects a number of different metrics via its intuitive real-time collection interface. Using this data, a leaderboard as well as user statistics is readily available for users to see.

2.1.2 Beer Pong Rules

Materials Required

  • 1 table no less than 7 feet (2 m) long and no less than 2 feet ( m) wide.
  • 12 red Solo cups
  • 2 ping pong balls
  • Any beverage

Setting Up

  • Twelve cups are arranged as shown below.
  • Fill each cup a quarter of the way full with the beverage you have chosen.
  • Players stand at the short ends of the table on their team's respective side.

Gameplay

  • To start the game, Eye-to-eye determines the first team to shoot.
    • One person from each team begins the process. After each failed attempt, the two team members swap places.
    • If both parties hit their eye-to-eye in a given turn, eye-to-eye resumes until only one team scores.
  • Players take turns attempting to throw the ping pong ball into the cups of the other team. A successfully sunk cup results in that cup being removed.
  • If both members of a team sink their cups in one turn, they shoot again.
  • If after a shot, the ball spins in the rim of the opponents' cup, the defending team may attempt to blow the ball out of the cup. If the ball is ejected and remains dry, no cup is scored.
  • Air balls result in a loss of the current turn.
    • If the first shooter Air Balls then the second shooter must forfeit his/her shot.
    • If the second shooter Air Balls then play resumes as though the turn ended normally.
  • If a shot rolls/bounces back to the shooting team on the table (never once touching the ground) and the shooting team gains possesion, a member may attempt a trick shot.
  • Each team is entitled to one re-rack at any point throughout the game.
  • Bounce shots count as two cups scored, the cup hit, as well as one selected by the offending team.
    • The defending team may attempt to disrupt the ball once it has made contact with the table.
  • If two non-bounce shots hit the same cup, the cup hit, as well as one selected by the scoring team are counted and removed.
  • Any knocking over of a cup is considered a Party Foul. All disrupted cups are to be removed and are considered scored by the other team.
  • If the ball is disrupted by the defending team (BEFORE it has made contact with the table) a cup is lost by the defending team. This is considered a Party Foul

Ending a Game

  • If a cup in the hand of a player is sunk, the scoring team is automatically victorious and the 'Game Over' option is selected.
    • Losing team does not qualify for redemption
  • If all the cups on one side are sunk, that team is eligible for Redemption.
    • The objective of redemption is to sink the remaining cups on the other team's side.
    • Both players on the redeeming team keep shooting until they miss.
    • If one player misses, the other may continue to shoot.
  • If both players sink the last remaining cup, or it is a bounce, the defending team is not eligible for redemption.

2.1.3 Product Usage

Users can search other users and challenge them to a game. Only one user, from one team is responsible for the operation of the interface. The interface offers a simulation of a beer pong game. The user selects the cup that was sunk, then the action, then the user who performed the action. Before the game is valid, a confirmation from one member of the team who wasn't tracking the game is required.

Pong Tracker can view their own statistics as well as other users'. A leaderboard is available to define the strongest users.

2.2 Actors and Use Cases

Actors

The following is a list of actors that are associated with Pong Tracker. Actors described here are illustrated in the Use Case Diagram shown above.

  • Administrator - an individual who has special privileges within the website. An administrator can login, logout, add an institution, delete an institution, ban a user, and unban a user. Administrators and end user accounts are completely separate.
  • User - an individual who uses the website for active game play. In terms of game play, the end user may create, score or confirm a game. Users can view the entire leaderboard, login, logout, inactivate their account, view their personal statistics, and view other players' statistics.
  • Guest - an individual who is not using the website for active game play. A guest may view a limited version of the leaderboard and may register. Once a guest has registered, they become a user.

Use Cases

The following is a list of use cases that are associated with the actors listed above. The use cases described here are illustrated in the Use Case Diagram shown above.

  • Admin Login - a separate page from where a user logs in. An administrator must provide their username and password to authenticate their identity
  • Admin Logout - an administrator need only select the "Logout" option to have access to their account session terminated
  • Add Institution - an administrator can add institutions that guests select when registering to become a user.
  • Delete Institution - an administrator can delete institutions that guests select when registering to become a user.
  • Ban User - an administrator can ban a user who has not conformed to the website's rules or policies
  • Unban User - an administrator can lift a ban on a user who has not confirmed to the website's rules or policies
  • User Login - a separate page from where an administrator logs in where a user must provide their username and password as discussed in section to authenticate their identity
  • User Logout - a user need only select the "Logout" option to have access to their account session terminated
  • Create Game - a user can create a beer pong game
  • Score Game - once a beer pong game has been created, a user uses the interface to record the results of the game as it is being played
  • Confirm Game Results - once a game has been completed, a user must confirm the results of the game in order for the game data in question to be used and considered in the statistics and leaderboard areas
  • View Personal Statistics - a user may view their personal statistics at any time given they are logged in. The statistics shown here include only games that have been confirmed by players involved in a particular game.
  • View Other Users' Statistics - a user may view other players' personal statistics at any time given they are logged in. The statistics shown here include only games that have been confirmed by players involved in a particular game.
  • Delete Account - a user can delete their account at any time
  • View Rules - a guest or user can view the rules of pong at any given time
  • View Full Leaderboard - a user may view the entire leaderboard at any time given they are logged in. The statistics shown here include only games that have been confirmed by players involved in a particular game.
  • Register - a guest must provide personal information, including at minimum a username, password and institution, in order to facilitate the login functionality
  • View Limited Leaderboard - a guest can see the top ten players on the website. For full access to the leaderboard, a guest must become a user

3 External Interface Requirements

3.1 User Interface

The user interface must be designed to be engaging and simple for the user. The user interface must emphasize the display of user statistics, since this is a key feature for our competitive user base. To make this possible, both a desktop and a mobile form factor of the website are to be developed.

  • The user interface for the Beer Pong network is a dynamic website.
  • Users log in, and the content changes according to their account level (user vs. admin).
  • Users see a front page with their current statistics. Users create a new game.
  • The function of the interface is to input data about the game into the database as easily and intuitively as possible.
  • The game interface is entirely visual and engaging in order to simulate the experience of a beer pong game.

3.1.1 Desktop Website

The desktop version sees more usage by users and guests in their free time since it is unlikely that they have their laptop with them during agame of beer pong.

  • Greater range of leaderboard data
  • More features to connect with and browse other users
  • Access to personal account administrative features

3.1.2 Mobile Website

The mobile version, for the most part, affects the users who are currently participating in a beer pong game and are therefore interacting with the main product features.

  • Highly reactive, intuitive, and well-designed user interface to facilitate data entry
  • Less emphasis on full set of social networking capabilities
  • Limited delivery of statistics

3.2 Software Interface

  • Pong Tracker operates on modern, standard internet browsers (Internet Explorer 9/10, Firefox, Google Chrome, and Safari).
  • The system uses a relational database.

Statistical Algorithm Library

  • The library allows developers to specify what inputs go into the algorithm. The algorithm then calculates the user's skill level in comparison to other users.
  • Documentation is available at GitHub TrueSkill Readme, TrueSkill Website, and Elo Rating System Documentation.
  • We are permitted to use the algorithm.

Login Methods

  • Pong Tracker implements third party login methods.
  • These methods allow a user to login into the website with an existing account from another website.
  • These website provide libraries which developers use to obtain a user's profile information.
  • Possible tools are The OpenID and Facebook Login for Web

3.3 Communications Interface

The underlying programming language and the database are located on a server; the user's device handles some computation.

4 System Features

4.1 Profile

Description

Guests register in order to play games and record statistics. Guests have the option to create an account directly on the website, or to register using a third party login. A guest becomes a user once they have registered. The user profile page displays personal information and statistics.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

From the main page, by selecting login, a user can login to the website, or through the third party login.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 Only one user is in a session at one time

4.2 Create a Game

Description

Games are the main feature of the website.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

A new game is created by a user. A game consists of four players. When a new game is created, the user who created the game, selects and adds other players to the game, up to a maximum of 3. A user may add a guest as a player. Note that the guest does not have their statistics recorded, nor can they interact with the game user interface. When three players are selected, the game starts, and the user who created the game sees the game interface.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 The user who creates a game must be registered.
  • REQ-2 The user who creates the game must not be in another game at the same time.
  • REQ-3 There must be at least two users in a game.
  • REQ-4 Each team must have at least one user.

4.3 Play a Game

Description

During a game, a user interacts with the game user interface. Actions in the physical game are recorded in the game user interface by selecting a player, a cup, and the applicable action. This is required for advancement of the game.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

Actions by a player, associated with specific cups, are selected throughout the game, as they are done in the physical game.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 Only one cup is selected at a time.
  • REQ-2 Only one action per cup can be selected.
  • REQ-3 Only one player can be associated with an action.

4.4 Validate a Game

Description

At the conclusion of a game, a game must be confirmed for validity.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

At the end of a game, one user from the opposing team must confirm results of the game. Once the game is confirmed, it is considered completely finished, and statistics are calculated and recorded. A team/user may deny validity in which case the game is not factored into statistics.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 One user from the opposing team must confirm validity for a game to be included in the statistics.
  • REQ-2 A team/user can deny the validity of a game.

4.5 Statistics and Leaderboard

Description

Statistics are viewable throughout the website. Statistics are calculated by the server and are stored in a database. Statistics are recorded for users only. The leaderboard is displayed on the main page of the website and is viewable to users. A limited leaderboard consisting of the top ten players is viewable to guests. Personal and other user statistics are viewable by users only. Statistics are searchable by user.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

At the end of a game, the Pong Tracker calculates and records users' statistics. Going to the user profile page displays the user's personal statistics. The user profile page has the functionality to search for other users in order to view their statistics. Going to the main page displays a leaderboard.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 A game must finish completely and be confirmed by all necessary players before statistics are calculated and committed to the database.

5 Other Non-Functional Requirements

5.1 Performance Requirements

The front-end pages for Pong Tracker must be loaded quickly and seamlessly with little time between transitions to other pages, and the game interface's dialog boxes should take virtually no time to load. An initial user volume consisting of post-secondary students in Ontario is expected and as such the software must be built to handle this workload.

5.2 Security Requirements

For security, there are three requirements:

  • User Login Page
    This is the main login page for users to enter Pong Tracker in order to access their personal statistics and engage in game play. These users do not have access to any website changes and do not even know another login page exists for administrative users.
  • Administrator User Login Page
    A second login page exists that is invisible to users, where administrators enter Pong Tracker to make changes to sensitive information.
  • Password Security
    An encryption method must be in place to protect the passwords of users and administrators.

5.3 Software Quality Attributes

5.3.1 Maintainability

The client expects to be able to make changes to the website in the future therefore the website must be easy to maintain and well-documented.

5.3.2 Scalability

The website must have the potential to be expanded to handle larger amounts of users should it become more popular.
