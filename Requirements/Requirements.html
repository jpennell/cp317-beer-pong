1.4 Audience

The targeted audience for this product is Beer Pong players. Players must have access to the internet, and must attend an Ontario, post-secondary institution. Each team consists of at least one two users. A user is registered with Pong Tracker. A guest is allowed to participate in a game. A guest is not registered with Pong Tracker.

1.5 Product Scope

Pong Tracker is a website that provides users the chance to record and view statistics of Beer Pong games played by themselves and other Pong Tracker users. The website allows a user to enter every detail of the game they are playing in real time. The scope of this project includes both a desktop , as well as a mobile oriented website. The scope does not include native applications for any mobile device.

1.5.1 Value to Society

Pong Tracker allows users to come together to record and share their Beer Pong statistics. Pong Tracker feeds the users' need to stay connected, meet new people, and creates an opportunity to share their interest of the game Beer Pong.

1.5.2 Value to Individuals

Pong Tracker creates a friendly, competitive and entertaining environment for every user and guest involved.

2 Product Description

This section describes the specifics of Pong Tracker.

2.1 Product Perspective

2.1.1 Game Requirements

Listed are the materials for a game of Beer Pong, as well as the way to set up a game.

2.1.1.1 Materials Required

  • 1 table no less than 7 feet (2 m) long and no less than 2 feet (0.6 m) wide.
  • 12 red Solo cups.
  • 2 ping pong balls.
  • Any beverage.

2.1.1.2 Setting Up the Game

  • Choose teams. Each team consists of two players.
  • Each team sets up six red Solo cups, as in Diagram 2.1.
  • Fill each cup a quarter of the way full.
  • Players stand at the ends of the table, behind their team's cups.

2.1.2 Beer Pong Rules

Listed are the rules for a game of Beer Pong, as assumed by Pong Tracker. Rules may be modified by the teams playing.

2.1.2.1 Gameplay

  • To determine the first team to shoot, players participate in eye-to-eye. Eye-to-eye consists of a member from each team shooting simultaneously at the opposing team's cups while making full eye contact with the opposing shooter.
    • One person from each team begins. After each failed attempt, the two team members swap places.
    • When a ball is successfully sunk, the scoring team is awarded the first shot. The cup that is sunk is not counted during eye-to-eye.
    • If both teams hit their eye-to-eye in a given turn, eye-to-eye resumes until only one team scores.
  • Teams take turns attempting to throw the ping pong ball into the cups of the opposing team. The team who is currently shooting is called the shooting team. The team who is being shot at is called the defending team.
  • When a ball has been sunk in a cup, that cup is removed from the table by the defending team. The defending team must drink the contents of that cup in a timely fashion (see Ending a Game).
  • If both members of the shooting team sink any two of the defending team's cups in one turn, they shoot again.
  • After a shot, the ball spins in the rim of the defending team's cup, the defending team can attempt to blow the ball out of the cup. If the ball is ejected and remains dry, no cup is removed.
  • An air ball is a shot that does not hit the table, nor any cup on the table.
    • If the first shooter air balls, then the second shooter must forfeit their shot.
    • If the second shooter air balls, then play resumes as though the turn ended normally.
  • If the ball rolls or bounces back to the shooting team on the table (never once touching the ground), a member of the shooting team can attempt a trick shot.
    • A trick shot is any shot containing either multi-directional trajectory or non-standard origination.
      • Bouncing the ball off an adjacent wall.
      • Throwing the ball facing the opposite direction of the table.
    • A trick shot does not follow the standard rules for shooting, such as air ball.
    • A trick shot can not be disrupted by the defending team.
  • Each team is entitled to one re-rack at any point during the game. A re-rack is a request to shuffle the orientation of the remaining cups of the opposing team.
  • A bounce shot counts as two cups sunk. A bounce shot hits the table before entering a cup. The sunk cup and another chosen by the scoring team are removed.
    • The defending team can attempt to disrupt the ball after it has made contact with the table.
  • If two non-bounce shots hit the same cup, then the sunk cup, and another selected by the scoring team are removed.
  • A party foul is any infringement on the rules of the game. In particular:
    • Any knocking over of a cup is considered a party foul. All disrupted cups are to be removed and are considered scored on by the opposing team.
    • If the ball is disrupted in any way (that is not otherwise stated in the above rules) by the defending team, a cup is removed from the defending team and is considered scored on by the shooting team.

2.1.2.2 Ending a Game

  • A game is over when a team's cups are all removed from the table.
  • Before the game is complete, the team with no remaining cups is eligible for redemption.
    • If a team is sucessful, then the game resumes, with each team having three cups.
    • The objective of redemption is to sink the opposing team's remaining cups.
    • Both players on the redeeming team keep shooting until they miss.
    • If one player misses, the other continues to shoot.
    • A team is not eligible for redemption if:
        • Both players on the shooting team sink the defending team's remaining cup.
        • The remaining cup is sunk via a bounce shot.
        • A cup in the hand of a player is sunk (for example, before the player drinks).
          • There is beverage remaining in the cup.
          • The scoring team is automatically victorious.

2.1.3 Product Usage

A user can search other users and challenge them to a game. Only one user, from one team, is responsible for the operation of the interface. The interface offers a simulation of a Beer Pong game. The user selects the cup that was sunk, then the action, followed by the user who performed the action. Before the game is valid, a confirmation from one member of the team who wasn't tracking the game is required. A leaderboard is available to showcase the strongest users.

2.1.4 Website Rules

The following are the website rules for Pong Tracker. Any non-compliance can result in a user ban.

  • No fictitious accounts. A fictitious account does not represent a human being.
  • No joint accounts. A joint account attempts to represent more than one human being.
  • No recording more than thirty games in a twenty four hour period.
  • No malicious activity.

2.2 Actors and Use Cases

2.2.1 Actors

The following is a list of actors that are associated with Pong Tracker. Actors described here are illustrated in the Use Case Diagram shown above.

  • Administrator - an individual who has special privileges within the website. An administrator can login, logout, add an institution, delete an institution, ban a user, and unban a user. Administrator and user accounts are completely separate.
  • User - an individual who uses the website for active game play. A user can create, score or confirm a game, view the leaderboard, view rules, register, edit their profile, login, logout, delete their account, view their personal statistics, and view other users' statistics.
  • Guest - an individual who is not using the website for active game play. A guest can view a limited version of the leaderboard, register and view the rules. Once a guest has registered, they become a user.

2.2.2 Use Cases

The following is a list of use cases that are associated with the actors listed above. The use cases described here are illustrated in the Use Case Diagram shown above.

  • Administrator Login - a separate page from where a user logs in. An administrator must provide their username and password to authenticate their identity.
  • Administrator Logout - an administrator selects the "Logout" option to have their session terminated.
  • Add Institution - an administrator can add institutions that a user guest selects when registering.
  • Delete Institution - an administrator can delete institutions that a user guest selects when registering.
  • Ban User - an administrator can ban a user who has not conformed to Pong Tracker's rules.
  • Unban User - an administrator can lift a ban on a user who has not conformed to Pong Tracker's rules.
  • User Login - a separate page from where an administrator logs in. A user must provide their username and password to authenticate their identity.
  • User Logout - a user selects the "Logout" option to have their session terminated.
  • Create Game - a user can start tracking a Beer Pong game.
  • Score Game - following Create Game, a user interacts with the interface to record the results of the game as it is being played.
  • End Game - the user scoring a Beer Pong game can end it.
  • Confirm Game - once a game has been completed, a user from the team who was not tracking must confirm the results of the game.
  • View Personal Statistics - a user can view their personal statistics at any time given they are logged in. The statistics shown here include only games that have been confirmed.
  • View Other Users' Statistics - a user can view other users' personal statistics at any time given they are logged in. The statistics shown here include only games that have been confirmed.
  • Delete Account - a user can delete their account at any time. The user must provide their password to delete their account.
  • View Rules - a guest or user can view the rules of Beer Pong, and Pong Tracker's rules at any given time.
  • View Full Leaderboard - a user can view the entire leaderboard at any time given they are logged in. The statistics shown here include only games that have been confirmed.
  • Register - a guest user must provide personal information in order to facilitate the login functionality.
  • View Limited Leaderboard - a guest can see the top ten users on the website. For full access to the leaderboard, a guest must become a user.

Pong Tracker is required to run on a Unix server (Hopper).

3 External Interface Requirements

3.1 User Interface

There is both a desktop and a mobile layout of the website. The user interface for Pong Tracker follows the following characteristics:

  • The user interface is dynamic; the content changes based on the users actions.
  • The user interface has a consistent look and feel throughout.
  • The user interface is simple and intuitive.
  • The user interface for mobile is designed for small screens.

3.2 Software Interface

Pong Tracker operates on modern, standard internet browsers.

  • Internet Explorer 9, 10
  • Firefox
  • Google Chrome
  • Safari

The system uses a relational database.

3.2.1 Statistical Algorithm Library

  • The library allows developers to specify what inputs go into the algorithm. The algorithm then calculates the users' skill level in comparison to other users.
  • Documentation is available at GitHub TrueSkill Readme, TrueSkill Website, and Elo Rating System Documentation.

3.2.2 Login Methods

  • Pong Tracker implements third party login methods.
  • These methods allow a user to login into the website with an existing account from another website.
  • These website provides libraries which developers use to obtain user profile information.
  • Possible tools are The OpenID and Facebook Login for Web

3.3 Communications Interface

The website and server system use multiple protocols for communicating over the internet. Described here are some of the application layer protocols.

  • Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) - the standard communication protocol that is the foundation for the World Wide Web.
  • Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure (HTTPS) - used for secure communication of passwords and other user data.
  • Transport Layer Securisty (TLS/SSL) - encrypted protocols that are fundemental in HTTPS.

3.4 Hardware Interface

Minimal hardware is needed for the use of Pong Tracker.

  • User
    • The user has a mouse and keyboard.
  • Administrator
    • Access to the server hardware is determined by the third party website host.

4 System Features

4.1 Profile

Description

A guest user registers in order to play games and record statistics. A guest user has the option to create an account directly on the website, or to register using a third party login. A guest becomes a user once they have registered. The user's profile page displays personal information and statistics.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

From the main page, by selecting login, a user can login to the website, or through the third party login.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 Only one user is in a session at one time.

4.2 Create a Game

Description

Games are the main feature of the website.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

A new game is created by a user. A game consists of four players. When a new game is created, the user who created the game, selects and adds other players to the game, up to a maximum of 3. A user may add a guest as a player. A guest cannot interact with the game user interface, however, their statistics are kept up to 2 days. When three players are selected, the game starts, and the user who created the game sees the game interface.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 The user who creates a game must be registered.
  • REQ-2 The user who creates the game must not be in another game at the same time.
  • REQ-3 There must be at least two users in a game.
  • REQ-4 Each team must have at least one user.

4.3 Score a Game

Description

During a game, a user interacts with the game user interface. Actions in the physical game are recorded in the game user interface by selecting a cup, an action, and the applicable player. This is required for advancement of the game.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

Actions by a player, associated with specific cups, are selected throughout the game, as they are done in the physical game.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 Only one cup is selected at a time.
  • REQ-2 Only one action per cup can be selected.

4.4 Validate a Game

Description

At the conclusion of a game, a game must be confirmed for validity.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

At the end of a game, one user from the team that does not score the game (as outlined in 4.3 Score a Game), must confirm results of the game. Once the game is confirmed, it is considered completely finished, and statistics are calculated and recorded. A user may deny validity in which case the game is not factored into statistics.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 One user from the team that does not score the game must confirm validity for a game to be included in the statistics.
  • REQ-2 A user can deny the validity of a game.

4.5 Statistics and Leaderboard

Description

Statistics are viewable throughout the website. Statistics are calculated by the server and are stored in a database. The leaderboard is displayed on the main page of the website and is viewable to users. A limited leaderboard consisting of the top ten users is viewable to guests. Personal and other user statistics are viewable by users only. Statistics are searchable by user.

Stimulus/Response Sequence

At the end of a game, Pong Tracker calculates and records users' statistics. Going to the user profile page displays the user's personal statistics. The user profile page has the functionality to search for other users in order to view their statistics.

Functional Requirements

  • REQ-1 A game must finish completely and be confirmed before statistics are calculated and committed to the database.

5 Other Non-Functional Requirements

5.1 Performance Requirements

The front-end pages for Pong Tracker must be loaded quickly and seamlessly with little time between transitions to other pages, and the game interface's dialog boxes should take virtually no time to load. An initial user volume consisting of post-secondary students in Ontario is expected and as such the software must be built to handle this workload.

5.2 Security Requirements

For security, there are three requirements:

  • User Login Page
    This is the main login page for a user to enter Pong Tracker in order to access their personal statistics and engage in game play. These users do not have access to any website changes.
  • Administrator Login Page
    A second login page exists that is invisible to users, where an administrator enters Pong Tracker to make changes to sensitive information.
  • Password Security
    An encryption method must be in place to protect the passwords of users and administrators.

5.3 Software Quality Attributes

5.3.1 Maintainability

The client expects to be able to make changes to the website in the future therefore the website must be easy to maintain and well-documented.

5.3.2 Scalability

The website must have the potential to be expanded to handle larger amounts of users should it become more popular.

Term Definition
Administrator An individual that is in charge of Pong Tracker.

Guest A player that is involved in a game, but is not registered with the website.
Party foul An infringement on the rules of the game, or knocking over a cup, spilling its contents.
